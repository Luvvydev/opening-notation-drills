Why openings disappear during games

Opening memory is usually weak because the player recognizes the line while reading it, then cannot produce it at the board. The fix is recall practice. You need to see the position, choose the move, and repeat the position until the move becomes easier to find.

The basic loop

  1. Pick one opening.
  2. Play the moves from memory.
  3. Read the feedback after mistakes.
  4. Repeat the same line later.

What to avoid

Do not add ten openings at once. A smaller repertoire drilled repeatedly is easier to use than a large file of lines you cannot remember during a game.
