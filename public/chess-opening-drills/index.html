Chess opening drills for players who forget lines during games

Built for recall

You play the move, get feedback, and repeat the line until the pattern is easier to find in a real game.

What makes a useful drill

A useful chess opening drill forces you to remember the move from the position. It should also explain mistakes so the next attempt is less random.

Drill flow

Choose an opening, play through a line, fix mistakes, then come back to the same line later. That loop is more practical than reading more theory without testing yourself.

Who this helps

These drills are aimed at players who know some opening names but still lose the thread after a few moves.
