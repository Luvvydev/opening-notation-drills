Rousseau Gambit

The Rousseau Gambit appears after 1.e4 e5 2.Nf3 Nc6 3.Bc4 f5. Black is immediately asking White whether the bishop on c4 and the center are ready for a sharp game.

  • Immediate initiative
  • Theory: Medium
  • Account required

How the opening starts

Black is spending a pawn or weakening move to seize attacking chances before White settles. The line is very practical because many White players enter it without preparation and have to solve concrete problems right away.

Move order preview
1.e4 e5 2.Nf3 Nc6 3.Bc4 f5 4.d3 Nf6 5.Nc3

What matters early

  • Black needs quick development and active piece play for the pawn investment to make sense.
  • The opening is concrete. Move accuracy matters more than broad principles in many lines.
  • White usually tries to keep the extra material while defusing the initiative.

What usually goes wrong

  • If the attack stalls, Black is left with structural damage and no compensation.
  • Loose kingside play can backfire immediately if White stays coordinated.
  • You need to know which tactical motifs actually work.
