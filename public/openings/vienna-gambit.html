Vienna Gambit

The Vienna Gambit combines 2.Nc3 with an early f4 and aims for fast initiative against the center and kingside. It keeps some Vienna flexibility while turning the game sharp very quickly.

  • Aggressive center break
  • Theory: Medium
  • Account required

How the opening starts

The usual route is 1.e4 e5 2.Nc3 Nf6 3.f4. White is spending a pawn tempo to gain space and open attacking lines. Black can respond in several ways, so practical line knowledge matters.

Move order preview
1.e4 e5 2.Nc3 Nf6 3.f4 d5 4.fxe5 Nxe4 5.d3 Nxc3 6.bxc3

What matters early

  • White is trying to make Black solve central and kingside questions at the same time.
  • The opening is strongest when White keeps development moving alongside the pawn play.
  • Typical compensation comes from activity, open files, and central pressure.

What usually goes wrong

  • Pushing f4 without development can just weaken the king side.
  • Black counterplay in the center is the main thing White has to respect.
  • Some branches require exact tactical play from move three onward.
