King's Indian Defense

The King’s Indian Defense lets White build a center, then tries to attack that center with piece pressure and pawn breaks. Black often castles quickly and plays for ...e5 or ...c5, depending on the setup.

  • Hypermodern counterplay
  • Theory: High
  • Free

How the opening starts

The main move order is 1.d4 Nf6 2.c4 g6 3.Nc3 Bg7 4.e4 d6. Black is committing to a fianchetto structure and a counterattacking middlegame. The resulting positions can be very dynamic even when the first moves look quiet.

What matters early

  • The central break choice changes the whole game, especially ...e5 versus ...c5 structures.
  • Kingside attacking plans are common, but they only work if the center is under control.
  • Piece coordination is more important than grabbing space early.

What usually goes wrong

  • If Black mis-times the pawn breaks, White can squeeze the position with space alone.
  • The opening can become strategically difficult if you only know tactical motifs.
  • Some lines require very concrete move order knowledge.
