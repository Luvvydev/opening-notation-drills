Scandinavian Defense

The Scandinavian meets 1.e4 with 1...d5 and forces an immediate central decision. Black often develops the queen early, then aims for a compact and familiar structure.

  • Direct center strike
  • Theory: Low to medium
  • Account required

How the opening starts

After 1.e4 d5 2.exd5 Qxd5, Black trades some queen time for a very direct challenge to the center. Many players like the opening because the structures repeat often and the first phase is easy to reach in blitz and rapid games.

Move order preview
1.e4 d5 2.exd5 Qxd5 3.Nc3 Qa5 4.d4 c6 5.Nf3 Bg4

What matters early

  • The queen move is acceptable if it leads to fast development and a clear structure.
  • Black usually wants ...c6, ...Nf6, and active bishop squares soon after the queen retreat.
  • The opening works best when Black knows which queen square fits the branch.

What usually goes wrong

  • If Black wastes time after the queen move, White gets a free lead in development.
  • White will often try to gain tempi by hitting the queen while taking space.
  • The opening is practical, but it still needs exact early move order.
