Sicilian Defense

The Sicilian Defense starts with 1...c5 and accepts an uneven structure from move one. Black gives up symmetry to get active piece play and queenside counterplay.

  • Counterattacking
  • Theory: High
  • Free
Open trainer Browse all guides

How the opening starts

After 1.e4 c5, Black is contesting d4 without mirroring White. In open Sicilians, Black often attacks the center with ...d6 or ...e6, develops ...Nf6, and uses the c file plus queenside space as long term counterplay.

Move order preview
1.e4 c5 2.Nf3 d6 3.d4 cxd4 4.Nxd4 Nf6 5.Nc3 a6

What matters early

  • The c file and the d5 square usually matter more than material trivia in the opening.
  • A lot of Sicilian positions are decided by whether Black gets ...d5 cleanly.
  • Piece placement changes by variation, so broad structure understanding matters more than memorizing isolated move numbers.

What usually goes wrong

  • If Black drifts and never challenges the center, White can just keep space and attack first.
  • Move order mistakes can get you hit by Bb5 lines, anti Sicilians, or direct kingside pressure.
  • The opening punishes vague play. You need a branch choice and a plan.

French Defense

Petrov Defense

The Petrov Defense replies to 1.e4 e5 2.Nf3 with 2...Nf6 and often heads into symmetrical positions where accuracy matters more than excitement.
