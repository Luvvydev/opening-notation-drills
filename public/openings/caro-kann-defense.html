Caro-Kann Defense

The Caro-Kann meets 1.e4 with 1...c6 and usually follows with ...d5. Black challenges the center early while keeping the light squared bishop flexible.

  • Solid structure
  • Theory: Medium

How the opening starts

After 1.e4 c6 2.d4 d5, Black is asking White to define the center. In many lines Black accepts a slightly passive first phase in exchange for a compact structure and a straightforward middlegame plan.

Move order preview
1.e4 c6 2.d4 d5 3.Nc3 dxe4 4.Nxe4 Bf5

What matters early

  • The light squared bishop often develops before ...e6, which is a major reason people choose this defense.
  • Black usually wants controlled central exchanges, not random complications.
  • Endgames often suit Black if the opening phase stays sound.

What usually goes wrong

  • If Black misplaces the queen knight or bishop, the position can get cramped for no reason.
  • White often uses space and fast development to make Black prove the setup works.
  • You still need a branch choice against Advance, Exchange, and Classical setups.
