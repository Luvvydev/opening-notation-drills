Fried Liver Attack

The Fried Liver Attack appears after White jumps a knight into f7 in the Two Knights Defense. It is concrete, tactical, and very easy to mishandle if either side is guessing.

  • Tactical attack
  • Theory: Medium
  • Free

How the opening starts

The core sequence is 1.e4 e5 2.Nf3 Nc6 3.Bc4 Nf6 4.Ng5 d5 5.exd5 Nxd5 6.Nxf7. White gives up time and often a knight retreat route in exchange for king exposure and tactical pressure.

What matters early

  • This opening is about calculation. General principles alone will not save bad moves.
  • White is trying to use the exposed king and loose black pieces before Black consolidates.
  • The attack is dangerous because Black often has only a few accurate defensive moves.

What usually goes wrong

  • White can overestimate the attack and end up down material with no follow up.
  • Black usually survives with precise defense. One active move can change the evaluation fast.
  • Memorizing only the first trap is not enough.
