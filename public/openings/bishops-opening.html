Bishop's Opening

The Bishop’s Opening develops the bishop to c4 on move two and keeps several transposition routes available. It is practical, light on theory compared with major e4 openings, and still asks clear questions of Black.

  • Flexible e4 line
  • Theory: Low to medium

How the opening starts

White begins 1.e4 e5 2.Bc4. The bishop targets f7 immediately and leaves the knight on g1 free to support different setups. Some branches resemble the Italian, while others stay more independent.

Move order preview
1.e4 e5 2.Bc4 Nf6 3.d3 c6 4.Nf3 d5 5.Bb3

What matters early

  • The opening is flexible, so choose a plan rather than making neutral waiting moves.
  • Early pressure on f7 matters mainly because it influences Black’s development choices.
  • White often wants d3, Nf3, and c3 or Nc3 depending on the response.

What usually goes wrong

  • Moving the bishop out early does not justify ignoring development.
  • If White mis-times the center, Black can equalize with ...d5.
  • Because it is less theoretical, many positions still require original thought.

Position snapshot

A flexible Bishop’s Opening structure after White steps the bishop back and keeps central options open.
