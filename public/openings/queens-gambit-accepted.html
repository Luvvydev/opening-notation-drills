Queen's Gambit Accepted

The Queen’s Gambit Accepted takes the c4 pawn and asks White to prove compensation through development and central pressure. Black tries to return the pawn under decent conditions or hold it briefly while finishing development.

  • Direct central test
  • Theory: Medium
  • Free
Open trainer Browse all guides

How the opening starts

After 1.d4 d5 2.c4 dxc4, Black gives up the central pawn duo but gains time to challenge White’s center later. White usually gets active pieces and easy development, so Black needs precise moves rather than casual pawn holding.

Move order preview
1.d4 d5 2.c4 dxc4 3.Nf3 Nf6 4.e3 e6 5.Bxc4 c5

What matters early

  • The opening is about time and structure, not greed.
  • Black often meets White’s center with ...c5 or ...e5 once development catches up.
  • Piece activity is the real compensation White is trying to convert.

What usually goes wrong

  • Trying to keep the pawn at any cost is often just wrong.
  • Black can fall behind in development very quickly after one slow move.
  • White still has to prove the initiative. Easy moves do not mean automatic advantage.
