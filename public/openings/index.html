White openings and attacks

These pages cover practical White repertoires, attacking tries, and a few offbeat entries that still reach coherent positions.

White opening • Free

London System

The London System gives White a familiar structure with early Bf4, solid central support, and plans that stay usable across a lot of Black move orders.

Stable setup Low to medium
White opening • Free

Ruy Lopez

The Ruy Lopez develops naturally and places immediate pressure on the e5 pawn through the knight on c6. A lot of its value comes from understanding plans after the opening, not from landing one early trick.

Classical pressure Medium to high
White opening • Free

Italian Game

The Italian Game leads to open piece play, early control of the center, and very common attacking themes against f7 and the kingside.

Open development Medium
White attack • Free

Fried Liver Attack

The Fried Liver Attack appears after White jumps a knight into f7 in the Two Knights Defense. It is concrete, tactical, and very easy to mishandle if either side is guessing.

Tactical attack Medium

English Opening

The English Opening begins with 1.c4 and often aims at d5 control, queenside space, and transpositions into a wide range of structures.

Flank control Medium
White opening • Account required

Bishop's Opening

The Bishop’s Opening develops the bishop to c4 on move two and keeps several transposition routes available. It is practical, light on theory compared with major e4 openings, and still asks clear questions of Black.

Flexible e4 line Low to medium
White gambit • Account required

Danish Gambit

The Danish Gambit gives up central pawns to open lines and flood the board with active pieces. White is betting that development and diagonals will matter more than material in the short term.

Open-file attack Medium
White gambit • Account required

King's Gambit

The King’s Gambit offers the f pawn after 1.e4 e5 2.f4 and tries to open the kingside before Black is ready. The opening is old, sharp, and still very dangerous in practical play.

Direct kingside attack Medium
White opening • Account required

Scotch Game

The Scotch Game breaks the center with 1.e4 e5 2.Nf3 Nc6 3.d4. White is trying to open lines quickly and avoid some of the slower maneuvering from the Ruy Lopez.

Direct central play Medium
White opening • Account required

Vienna Game

The Vienna Game begins with 1.e4 e5 2.Nc3. White keeps several setups available, including quiet development and sharper f4 based play.

Flexible e4 system Low to medium
White gambit • Account required

Vienna Gambit

The Vienna Gambit combines 2.Nc3 with an early f4 and aims for fast initiative against the center and kingside. It keeps some Vienna flexibility while turning the game sharp very quickly.

Aggressive center break Medium
White opening • Account required

Van't Kruijs Opening

The Van’t Kruijs Opening starts with 1.e3 and keeps almost every central plan available. White is delaying commitment and trying to choose the structure after seeing Black’s setup.

Quiet flexible setup Low

Black defenses and counterattacks

These pages cover the main Black repertoires in the library, plus sharper gambits that are used for practical surprise value.

Black defense • Free

Sicilian Defense

The Sicilian Defense starts with 1...c5 and accepts an uneven structure from move one. Black gives up symmetry to get active piece play and queenside counterplay.

Counterattacking High
Black defense • Free

Caro-Kann Defense

The Caro-Kann meets 1.e4 with 1...c6 and usually follows with ...d5. Black challenges the center early while keeping the light squared bishop flexible.

Solid structure Medium
Black defense • Free

French Defense

The French Defense challenges 1.e4 with 1...e6 and ...d5. Black often accepts less space early, then tries to undermine White’s center with piece pressure and pawn breaks.

Closed center Medium
Black defense • Free

Queen's Gambit Declined

The Queen’s Gambit Declined keeps the center intact and leads to structures that have been studied for a long time. Black does not accept the c4 pawn and focuses on stable development.

Classical structure Medium
Black defense • Free

Queen's Gambit Accepted

The Queen’s Gambit Accepted takes the c4 pawn and asks White to prove compensation through development and central pressure. Black tries to return the pawn under decent conditions or hold it briefly while finishing development.

Direct central test Medium
Black defense • Free

King's Indian Defense

The King’s Indian Defense lets White build a center, then tries to attack that center with piece pressure and pawn breaks. Black often castles quickly and plays for ...e5 or ...c5, depending on the setup.

Hypermodern counterplay High
Black defense • Account required

Scandinavian Defense

The Scandinavian meets 1.e4 with 1...d5 and forces an immediate central decision. Black often develops the queen early, then aims for a compact and familiar structure.

Direct center strike Low to medium
Black defense • Account required

Petrov Defense

The Petrov Defense replies to 1.e4 e5 2.Nf3 with 2...Nf6 and often heads into symmetrical positions where accuracy matters more than excitement.

Symmetrical and sound Medium
Black countergambit • Account required

Vienna Gambit Counter

The Vienna Gambit Counter is Black’s practical answer to the Vienna Gambit. The point is to hit the center with ...d5 and make White justify the pawn push to f4.

Direct central counter Medium
Black gambit • Free

Englund Gambit

The Englund Gambit throws in 1...e5 against 1.d4 and invites White to enter an unusual position immediately. It is mostly a practical weapon built on initiative and unfamiliarity.

Practical surprise Low to medium
Black gambit • Account required

Rousseau Gambit

The Rousseau Gambit appears after 1.e4 e5 2.Nf3 Nc6 3.Bc4 f5. Black is immediately asking White whether the bishop on c4 and the center are ready for a sharp game.

Immediate initiative Medium
Black gambit • Free

Stafford Gambit

The Stafford Gambit comes from the Petrov after Black sacrifices a pawn for rapid development and tactical threats. It is popular in online play because the danger is immediate and many players misstep quickly.

Trap-heavy attack Medium
