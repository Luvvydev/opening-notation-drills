Stafford Gambit

The Stafford Gambit comes from the Petrov after Black sacrifices a pawn for rapid development and tactical threats. It is popular in online play because the danger is immediate and many players misstep quickly.

  • Trap-heavy attack
  • Theory: Medium
  • Free
Open trainer Browse all guides

How the opening starts

The common route is 1.e4 e5 2.Nf3 Nf6 3.Nxe5 Nc6. Black gives up a pawn to bring pieces out fast and generate threats against f2, c2, and the white king before White completes development.

Move order preview

What matters early

  • The gambit lives on initiative. Every tempo matters.
  • Black is usually playing for tactical motifs against f2 and along the king file.
  • White often neutralizes it by staying calm and completing development.

What usually goes wrong

  • If Black does not generate concrete threats, the missing pawn becomes the whole story.
  • White can often refute casual trap attempts with simple moves.
  • This opening is strongest when the tactical lines are actually known, not improvised.

Petrov Defense

Rousseau Gambit

The Rousseau Gambit appears after 1.e4 e5 2.Nf3 Nc6 3.Bc4 f5. Black is immediately asking White whether the bishop on c4 and the center are ready for a sharp game.
Black gambit

Englund Gambit

The Englund Gambit throws in 1...e5 against 1.d4 and invites White to enter an unusual position immediately. It is mostly a practical weapon built on initiative and unfamiliarity.
