King's Gambit

The King’s Gambit offers the f pawn after 1.e4 e5 2.f4 and tries to open the kingside before Black is ready. The opening is old, sharp, and still very dangerous in practical play.

  • Direct kingside attack
  • Theory: Medium
  • Account required
Open trainer Browse all guides

How the opening starts

White uses 2.f4 to challenge e5 and open the f file. If Black accepts, the game usually becomes a race between White’s initiative and Black’s extra pawn plus chances to stabilize.

Move order preview
1.e4 e5 2.f4 exf4 3.Nf3 g5 4.h4 g4 5.Ne5

What matters early

  • Rapid development matters more than pawn counting in the first phase.
  • Open lines toward f7 and the black king are the main source of compensation.
  • A lot of branches revolve around how Black handles the pawn on f4.

What usually goes wrong

  • White can create weaknesses around the own king very quickly.
  • If the attack stalls, the endgame is usually poor for White.
  • The opening still needs concrete preparation against major black setups.

Vienna Gambit

The Vienna Gambit combines 2.Nc3 with an early f4 and aims for fast initiative against the center and kingside. It keeps some Vienna flexibility while turning the game sharp very quickly.
