Vienna Gambit Counter

The Vienna Gambit Counter is Black’s practical answer to the Vienna Gambit. The point is to hit the center with ...d5 and make White justify the pawn push to f4.

  • Direct central counter
  • Theory: Medium
  • Account required
Open trainer Browse all guides

How the opening starts

The key move is 1.e4 e5 2.Nc3 Nf6 3.f4 d5. Black refuses to sit and absorb pressure. The counter works by challenging White’s center before White finishes kingside development.

Move order preview
1.e4 e5 2.Nc3 Nf6 3.f4 d5 4.fxe5 Nxe4 5.d3

What matters early

  • Black is using central timing to punish White for spending a tempo on f4.
  • If White is inaccurate, Black can seize the initiative outright.
  • Development still matters. The countergambit does not excuse random piece moves.

What usually goes wrong

  • Black can overestimate the counterplay and forget king safety.
  • White still has attacking chances if the center opens on favorable terms.
  • You need a concrete line choice after the first exchanges.
