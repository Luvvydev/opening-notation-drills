What this opening is about

The London System gives White a practical setup with fast development, stable pawn structure, and recurring middlegame plans. It is popular because it is easier to reuse across many black responses than highly theoretical e4 repertoires.

ChessDrills uses guided repetition so you can practice the move order, review common setups, and stop losing time in the opening. The goal is not to dump theory onto one page. The goal is to make the first phase of the game easier to remember under practical conditions.

If you are building a white repertoire and want a repeatable way to study positions, this opening page gives you a direct route into the trainer while still leaving enough text for search engines and new visitors to understand what the opening does.
