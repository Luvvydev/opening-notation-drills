French Defense

The French Defense challenges 1.e4 with 1...e6 and ...d5. Black often accepts less space early, then tries to undermine White’s center with piece pressure and pawn breaks.

  • Closed center
  • Theory: Medium

How the opening starts

After 1.e4 e6 2.d4 d5, the opening often becomes a fight over the e4 and d4 chain. The structure tells you a lot about what both sides should do, which is why French players usually study plans by pawn chain first.

Move order preview
1.e4 e6 2.d4 d5 3.Nc3 Nf6 4.Bg5 Be7

What matters early

  • Know which pawn break matters in your branch, usually ...c5 or ...f6 at the right time.
  • Piece placement is shaped by the locked center more than by quick tactics.
  • The light squared bishop problem is real and has to be managed consciously.

What usually goes wrong

  • If Black never challenges the center, White can just sit on the space advantage.
  • Passive bishop placement can leave Black with a bad version of the structure.
  • Move order details against Advance and Exchange lines still matter.

Sicilian Defense

The Sicilian Defense starts with 1...c5 and accepts an uneven structure from move one. Black gives up symmetry to get active piece play and queenside counterplay.

Scandinavian Defense
